# Airsell Cargo: Air Waybill (AWB) Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

This document outlines the mandatory steps for completing an Air Waybill (AWB) to ensure compliance with carrier requirements (e.g., Ethiopian Airlines) and international shipping standards.

## 1. Shipper & Consignee Information
* **Shipper's Name and Address:** Must match the commercial invoice exactly. Include a contact phone number.
* **Consignee's Name and Address:** Ensure the full legal name and destination contact details are provided to avoid delivery delays.

## 2. Essential Flight & Routing Details
* **Airport of Departure:** HGA (Hargeisa).
* **Routing to:** Specify the destination airport code (e.g., ADD for Addis Ababa, DXB for Dubai, or NBO for Nairobi).
* **Carrier:** Ethiopian Airlines (ET) or specified carrier.

## 3. Cargo Description & Rating
* **Number of Pieces:** Count must match the physical cargo and packing list.
* **Gross Weight:** Must be weighed on calibrated scales in kilograms (kg).
* **Nature and Quantity of Goods:** Provide a clear description (e.g., "General Cargo - Spare Parts"). Avoid vague terms like "Samples."
* **Dimensions:** Length x Width x Height (cm) for each piece to calculate Volumetric Weight.

## 4. Financial & Legal Requirements
* **Chargeable Weight:** Use the greater of Actual Weight vs. Volumetric Weight ($L \times W \times H / 6000$).
* **Currency:** Typically USD for international freight.
* **Handling Information:** Note any special requirements (e.g., "Fragile," "Keep Cool").

## 5. Final Verification
* **Signature of Issuing Carrier/Agent:** Must be signed by the authorized Airsell Cargo representative.
* **Date of Execution:** The date the cargo is accepted for carriage.
